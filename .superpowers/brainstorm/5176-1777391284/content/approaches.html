3 Approaches to UofT Branding

How deeply should the branding be applied? Pick the direction that feels right.

A

Colour + Typography Refresh Recommended

Replace Inter/Playfair with Arial. Swap all grays for UofT Blue (#1E3765) and teal (#007894). Update buttons, step indicators, inputs, and map drawing elements. Light tinted section backgrounds. Subtle fade-in animations on steps. Clean, on-brand, low-risk.

UofT Blue buttons Teal section tints White cards + shadow
B

Full UofT Look — Dark Header

Option A plus a dark UofT Blue header bar at the top of the sidebar with the title in white, matching official UofT web properties. Stronger institutional feel. Accent colours used on step indicators and interactive elements. More visual weight, more "official".

Dark blue header Teal accents Sky blue highlights
C

Minimal Touch — Colours Only

Keep existing layout and font (Inter) but swap colours to UofT Blue and teal. Quickest to implement. Looks on-brand at a glance but doesn't fully meet the typography or animation guidelines. Good if you want a low-effort pass before a deeper overhaul later.

UofT colours only Keep Inter font No animations

My recommendation: Option A. It hits every major UofT guideline (colour, typography, cards, animations, accessibility) while keeping the sidebar clean and tool-focused. Option B is great if the project is being prominently co-branded with UofT on their own properties.
